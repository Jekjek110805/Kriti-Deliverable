
# Audience Research Framework v1

## Purpose

This framework defines how audience research is collected, evaluated, and transformed into actionable SEO insights.

The objective is not to collect random questions.

The objective is to identify:

- Real user pain points
    
- Buying signals
    
- Objections
    
- Comparison questions
    
- Decision-making criteria
    
- Frequently asked questions
    

that can improve topic selection and future content production.

---

# Core Principle

Keyword tools tell us:

```text
What people search.
```

Audience research tells us:

```text
Why they search.
```

The best opportunities combine both.

# Research Categories

Every question discovered must be classified.

---

## Category 1 — Pain Point

### Definition

User has a problem.

Examples:

```text
Why is our clinic struggling to track leads?

How do I manage patient follow-ups?
```

### Business Value

High

### Keep?

✅ Yes

---

## Category 2 — Buying Question

### Definition

User is actively evaluating solutions.

Examples:

```text
What is the best CRM for clinics?

Which CRM is easiest to implement?
```

### Business Value

Very High

### Keep?

✅ Yes

---

## Category 3 — Comparison Question

### Definition

User is comparing options.

Examples:

```text
HubSpot vs Salesforce

Monday vs Asana
```

### Business Value

Very High

### Keep?

✅ Yes

---

## Category 4 — Alternative Question

### Definition

User wants replacements.

Examples:

```text
Best HubSpot alternatives

Alternatives to Salesforce
```

### Business Value

Very High

### Keep?

✅ Yes

---

## Category 5 — Objection Question

### Definition

User has concerns preventing purchase.

Examples:

```text
Is HubSpot too expensive?

How difficult is CRM migration?
```

### Business Value

High

### Keep?

✅ Yes

---

## Category 6 — Beginner Education

### Definition

User is learning.

Examples:

```text
What is CRM?

How does CRM software work?
```

### Business Value

Medium

### Keep?

⚠ Depends on strategy

---

## Category 7 — General Discussion

### Definition

Conversation without clear SEO or business value.

Examples:

```text
Anyone else annoyed by software updates?

Random industry chat
```

### Business Value

Low

### Keep?

❌ No

|Signal Type|Score|
|---|--:|

|Comparison Question|5|
|Alternative Question|5|
|Pain Point|4|
|Objection Question|4|
|Beginner Question|2|
|General Discussion|0|

# Question Frequency Assessment

Questions repeated across sources indicate stronger demand.

---

- Reddit
    
- Quora
    
- PAA
    

Score:

```text
5
```

---

## Medium Frequency

Found in:

- Two sources
    

Score:

```text
3
```

---

## Low Frequency

Found once

Score:

```text
1
```

# Transformation Rules

Audience research must be converted into content assets.

---

## H2 Opportunities

Example:

Question:

```text
What CRM is easiest for clinics to implement?
```

Becomes:

```text
H2: Which CRM Is Easiest for Clinics to Implement?
```

---

## FAQ Opportunities

Example:

Question:

```text
How much does healthcare CRM software cost?
```

Becomes:

```text
FAQ:
How much does healthcare CRM software cost?
```

---

## Topic Opportunities

Example:

Repeated Question:

```text
Best CRM for Veterinary Clinics
```

Becomes:

```text
New Content Opportunity
```
